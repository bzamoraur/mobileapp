# H0 Playbook — Validate Viaje as a service (get to 3 paying advisors)

*The goal of H0 is **not** revenue and **not** a platform. It is to answer one question for the price of a few trips: **will travel professionals pay for a beautiful, offline, branded trip app for their clients?** If yes → build H1. If no → stop. Everything below serves that.*

**The one metric:** 3 advisors who *pay* (even a small amount) for a client trip app within ~8 weeks.
**Kill criteria:** <3 paying pilots; unwilling to pay >€20/trip or >€29/mo; or they love the demo but don't come back for a 2nd trip.

---

## 1. Who to target (ICP)

**Primary ICP — the boutique travel advisor / "travel designer".**
- 1–10 people. Sells **bespoke, premium leisure**: honeymoons, safaris, multigenerational family trips, luxury city breaks, tailor-made tours. Average trip ticket **€3,000+** (high ticket = the app's "wow" raises perceived value and helps them charge more / close more).
- **Already builds itineraries by hand** — Word/PDF/Canva/email, or a tool like Travefy/Wetu. They feel the pain of "my beautiful trip arrives as an ugly PDF."
- **Visible & reachable**: posts trips on Instagram/LinkedIn, has a website, cares about brand and client experience.
- Spain first (Spanish product), then LatAm (México, Colombia, Argentina), then the rest of Europe.

**Secondary ICP:** small DMCs / tour operators (sell to agencies or direct), honeymoon & wedding planners, experiential/adventure operators, "luxury travel curators".

**Special target — Umapped refugees.** Umapped (an advisor itinerary+app tool, owned by Flight Centre) **shut down Oct 2025**. Those advisors lost their client-app tool and are *actively shopping for a replacement right now*. This is the warmest cold audience that exists.

**Disqualify (don't waste time):** pure OTAs / transactional sellers, package-only agencies, mass-market high-volume/low-touch, anyone whose clients never get a written itinerary.

**Your unfair first lead:** **Rodrigo Peláez at Pangea** — the advisor who booked your own Tanzania trip. He is *exactly* the ICP, you're a happy client, and you already built him (effectively) a stunning app for a trip he sold. **Call him first.** Best-case he becomes design-partner #1 and refers two peers.

## 2. Where to find them (concrete, Spain-first)

**Your network (start here — warm always wins):**
- Anyone you know who used a travel advisor (honeymoon, safari, big family trip). Ask for the advisor's name + an intro.
- Rodrigo / Pangea (above). Ask him directly *and* for 2 referrals to independent advisors.

**Associations & directories (member lists = a target list):**
- **CEAV** (Confederación Española de Agencias de Viajes), **ACAVE**, **UNAV**, **AEDAVE**, **FEAAV**; regional associations. Their member directories are prospecting gold.
- Luxury networks/consortia present in Spain: **Virtuoso**, **Signature**, **Travel Leaders**, **Traveller Made**, **Forbes Travel Guide** advisors. (Membership lists/"find an advisor" pages = curated high-end ICP.)

**Events (face-to-face converts best for high-touch B2B):**
- **FITUR** (Madrid, late Jan) — the big one; advisors + operators in one hall.
- **B-Travel** (Barcelona), regional travel-trade workshops, fam-trip groups, advisor masterminds/meetups.

**Online (for cold + the Trojan-horse tactic in §5):**
- **Instagram**: search hashtags/bios — *#agentedeviajes #asesoradeviajes #traveldesigner #viajesalamedida #lunatdemiel #viajedenovios*. Advisors literally post the itineraries you can re-skin as a free demo.
- **LinkedIn**: titles "asesor/a de viajes", "travel designer", "agente de viajes", "tour operator"; filter by Spain + small company size. Sales Navigator if you have it.
- **Facebook groups / communities**: "Agentes de viajes España", advisor communities, Host Agency Reviews forums, Travel Weekly comment threads (find Umapped-shutdown discussions → name the refugees).
- **Their own websites** ("contacto" pages) — for the bespoke/luxury ones, a personal email + a free demo beats any form.

**Target list goal:** build a sheet of **40–60 named advisors** (name, agency, niche, IG/LinkedIn, email, a trip they recently sold). You only need ~3 to say yes; expect to touch many.

## 3. The offer (what you sell in H0)

> **"Te convierto la propuesta de viaje de tu cliente en una app preciosa, con tu marca, que se instala en el móvil y funciona sin conexión — lista en 48 h."**
> *("I turn your client's trip proposal into a beautiful, your-branded app that installs on their phone and works offline — ready in 48h.")*

**What's included (per trip):**
- You send the itinerary (or just the booking PDF / voucher) → you get back a **branded PWA link + a QR code** to hand the client.
- Their **logo + colours + agency contact** baked in (white-label).
- Everything the client needs in their pocket, **offline abroad**: day-by-day plan, map, hotels, flights, phrasebook with audio, practical/emergency info, a tailored "things to tick off" list (monuments / photos / dishes / wildlife), expenses, journal, packing.

**Pricing ladder (keep it stupid-simple):**
- **Pilot #1–2: free**, in exchange for (a) honest feedback, (b) a testimonial, (c) permission to use it as an anonymized case study. (You're buying *learning*, not revenue.)
- **Then: €49 per trip** (anchor; offer €39 "founding advisor" rate), **or** a **"founding advisor" plan: €99/month for unlimited trips** for the first 3 months. *(You must charge — a free pilot that never converts to paid teaches you nothing about WTP, which is the whole point of H0.)*
- Optional add-on to test ancillary WTP: **+€20** for "we also source 8–12 destination photos + a printable PDF keepsake."

**Why an advisor buys (the value story — sell the outcome, not the tech):**
- **Closes more / higher-ticket bookings:** the proposal *feels* premium → justifies their fee and price.
- **Differentiation:** their competitor sends a PDF; they send an app with their name on it.
- **Fewer "what's my hotel address / what time is the pickup" messages** mid-trip — it's all in the client's pocket, offline.
- **Repeat & referrals:** clients show the app to friends ("who made this?") → word of mouth for the advisor.

## 4. The numbers you're playing (so outreach feels sane)

Rough B2B reply funnel for *personalized* outreach: ~**30–40 quality touches → ~8–12 conversations → ~3–5 pilots → ~3 paid**. So: **build the 40–60 list, send ~40 personalized touches, expect ~10 calls.** The free-demo "Trojan horse" (next) materially lifts every step.

---

## 5. How to reach them — channels & the killer tactic

**Order of preference:** warm intro > a free demo of *their own* trip > personalized DM/email > generic cold.

**The Trojan horse (highest-converting move for a visual product):**
Pick an advisor, find a trip they publicly sell (their website/Instagram), and **build it as a free branded demo *before* contacting them.** Then open with the finished thing: *"I turned your Maldives honeymoon into this — it's yours, free, no strings. 30-sec look?"* Seeing their trip, with their brand, installable, is worth more than any pitch. **Make 5–10 of these** for your top targets. (This is exactly what the factory is for — each costs you minutes.)

**Cadence per target:** Touch 1 (DM or email with the demo) → Touch 2 (follow-up +3 days, add the airplane-mode angle) → Touch 3 (+5 days, "should I close your demo link or want it for a real client?"). Then stop and move on.

## 6. Scripts (paste-ready — Spanish first, English after)

### 6a. Cold DM / Instagram (short, value-first) — ES
> Hola [Nombre] 👋 Vi tu propuesta de [Maldivas/safari/…] y me encantó. Hago apps de viaje preciosas que se instalan en el móvil y funcionan **sin conexión** — con la marca de la agencia. Te he montado **gratis** un ejemplo con uno de tus viajes para que lo veas: [link]. ¿Te lo enseño en 5 min? Sin compromiso.

### 6b. Cold email — ES
> **Asunto:** La propuesta de [destino] de [Agencia], como una app (te dejo un ejemplo)
>
> Hola [Nombre],
> Soy [Tu nombre]. Ayudo a asesores de viajes a entregar sus itinerarios como una **app con su marca** que el cliente se instala en el móvil y usa **sin conexión** en destino (mapa, plan día a día, hoteles/vuelos, frases con audio, emergencias…).
>
> Para que lo veas en vez de contarlo, monté un ejemplo con [un viaje tuyo / un viaje tipo]: **[link]** (ábrelo en el móvil → "Añadir a pantalla de inicio").
>
> Si te encaja, te hago el de un cliente real en 48 h. El primero, gratis, a cambio de tu opinión sincera. ¿Te viene bien una llamada de 15 min esta semana?
>
> Un saludo, [Tu nombre] · [teléfono]

### 6c. Discovery call script (15 min)
1. **Rapport + qualify (4 min):** "Cuéntame cómo entregas hoy los itinerarios a tus clientes." Listen for: format (PDF/Word/tool), pain, average ticket, volume/month, who their client is (age/tech-comfort).
2. **Demo (5 min):** share a link live → *install to home screen* → *airplane mode on, it still works* → phrasebook **audio** → the tick-off **collection** → "todo esto, con tu marca."
3. **The offer (3 min):** "Te hago el de tu próximo cliente en 48 h. El primero gratis por tu feedback; luego 49 € por viaje o 99 €/mes ilimitado para los primeros que entran." 
4. **The ask (3 min):** "¿Tienes un viaje saliendo pronto que pueda montar esta semana?" Get a specific trip + the booking doc. If not now: "¿Te reservo tu demo y lo vemos cuando tengas uno?"

### 6d. Objection handling
- **"Ya uso Travefy/Word/Canva."** → "Genial para construir. Esto es lo que **recibe el cliente**: una app que funciona **sin conexión** en destino y lleva tu marca — no un PDF que se pierde en el email. Conviven."
- **"Mis clientes son mayores / poco tech."** → "Por eso se instala con un link y un QR, sin tienda de apps ni registro. Se abre como una foto. Te lo enseño con uno."
- **"¿Cuánto cuesta?"** → "El primero gratis por tu feedback; luego 49 €/viaje o 99 €/mes ilimitado para fundadores." (Hold price; don't discount fast.)
- **"¿Es seguro? Llevan datos del cliente."** → "Sí: acceso con código, no se indexa en Google, y borro los datos cuando quieras. (En H1 añadimos cuentas/acceso por email)."
- **"No tengo tiempo."** → "Cero trabajo para ti: me reenvías el PDF de la reserva y yo hago el resto. 48 h."
- **"¿Puede llevar mi marca/dominio?"** → "Sí — logo, colores y tus datos de contacto. Dominio propio en la versión de pago."

### 6e. English versions (for non-Spanish targets)
- **DM:** "Hi [Name] — loved your [Maldives] itinerary. I build beautiful trip apps that install on the phone and work **offline**, branded as the agency. I made you a **free** example with one of your trips: [link]. 5-min look, no strings?"
- **Umapped-refugee opener:** "Hi [Name] — saw Umapped is closing. I build branded, **offline** client trip apps generated from your itinerary in 48h — happy to migrate one of your trips free so you can compare. Want me to?"

## 7. Pilot mechanics (deliver the "wow", capture the learning)

1. **Intake:** one short form / email — destination, dates, the booking PDF or itinerary, logo + brand colour (optional), the client's first name. *Treat the booking doc as confidential.*
2. **Produce (you, with the factory):** `npm run new-trip` on a fresh repo → `import-travel-plan` (or `generate-from-destination`) → set their theme/branding → `fetch:images` → `npm run check` → deploy → send **link + QR**. (See `docs/RUNBOOK.md`.) Target **48h**.
3. **Hand-off:** give the advisor a 3-line "how to send it to your client" + the QR. Ask them to install it themselves first (they'll sell it better).
4. **Capture learning (the actual product of H0):** after the client travels (or after the advisor reviews), ask 5 questions — *Did it impress the client? Would you pay for the next one? What was missing? Would you want to make it yourself? What would you pay per month?* Write every answer down.
5. **Convert + refer:** "¿Montamos el del próximo cliente? (49 €)" and "¿Conoces a 1–2 asesores a quienes les encantaría?"

**Payment (keep it frictionless):** Stripe **Payment Link** or **Bizum**/transfer for one-offs; a Stripe subscription link for the €99/mo. Send a simple invoice. No contracts platform needed at this stage — a one-paragraph email T&C is enough (see §9).

## 9. Legal / privacy / promises (keep it light but real)

- **You will handle a client's personal trip data** (names, hotels, locators). Say so plainly: access-gated, `noindex`, deleted on request, not shared. One paragraph of T&C on the invoice covers H0; a proper DPA waits for H1.
- **Don't over-promise:** no bookings/payments inside the app yet; offline means *after first load on Wi-Fi*; you're a small service, not an enterprise SaaS (this is fine — advisors like dealing with a founder).
- **GDPR posture:** minimize what you store, delete after the trip + a short window, EU hosting (Cloudflare). Note it; don't gold-plate it in H0.

---

## 10. What "success" tells you (and what to watch)

- **Go to H1 if:** ≥3 advisors paid; ≥1 came back for a 2nd trip; the recurring theme in feedback is "I'd want to make these myself" (→ the editor/console is the product) **or** "give me more, I'll pay monthly" (→ subscription).
- **Pivot/stop if:** lots of "lovely!" but no payment; or they want it but only at ~€0; or the work per trip stays too manual to ever margin.
- **Listen hardest for:** which feature makes them gasp (offline? the audio phrasebook? the brand?), and whether **import-the-PDF** is the thing that saves them time — that single feature likely defines H1.

> Reminder: H0's deliverable is a **decision backed by evidence**, plus 3 paying logos and a stack of testimonials. Resist building the platform until the evidence is in.
